1940's

In the Begining . . .

A committee was appointed at the Layman’s Jubilee meeting to seek out a location for a Baptist Encampment Gound. On August 26, 1946, the committee recommended the purchase of 26.21 acres of land on the shores of Lake Brownwood at a price of $6,890. The grounds were dedicated in 1947 with a crowd of about 1500 people attending. Original kitchen facility moved to camp property from Camp Bowie Tabernacle constructed 60’x100’ of native rock, concrete and steel with a seating capacity of 1000 at a cost of $7,300. FBC Comanche built a large BBQ pit near the shore line Men of Downtown Bible Class in Brownwood built a tower just outside the tabernacle and installed a large bell with clapper. Water system was donated and 24 ft. water tower with 200 barrel water tank Bathhouse with shower and restroom facilities and septic tank was installed.

1950's

A home was built for a caretaker at a cost of $3,200. Girls Auxiliary and Royal Ambassador camps were started. Mr. & Mrs. Wild were employed as the first caretakers. In 1953, Mr. & Mrs. G.W. Howard took over as caretakers. H.A. Stephens of Brownwood built the first cabin and classroom. The first church to build a cabin was FBC Winters. T.M. Cash of Priddy, Texas built a cabin. WMU Lodge of District 16 was built at a cost of $6,000. Lodges were built by FBC Bangs, FBC Hamilton, FBC Santa Anna, and FBC Comanche. In order to provide all weather roads, crushed rock from Hall Mountain South of Brownwood was put down in 1952. Also, a concrete floor was put in the tabernacle and a pump pressure tank was installed for the water system. In 1952, a new addition to the dining room was built with screened sides and concrete floor to join the original kitchen. A 60’ x100’ swimming pool of concrete construction was built near the shoreline. Cabins were built by Lakeview Baptist Church, San Angelo and FBC DeLeon. In 1953, lodges were built by the following churches: FBC Brownwood, FBC San Angelo, Park Heights of San Angelo, FBC Early, FBC Eden, Mills County Association and Coleman County Association. In 1954, the following cabins were built: Cabins 2,3,4 & 5 were built by H.A. Stephens and were sold to Howard Payne College, Pumphrey Baptist Church, FBC Paint Rock, Melwood Baptist of Brownwood, Hopewell of Crews, FBC ElDorado. A cabin was moved to the grounds by Belle Plain Baptist of Brownwood. In 1955, cabins were built by FBC San Saba, FBC Ballinger, and Coggin Ave. Baptist of Brownwood. H.A. Stephens had 57 benches built for the camp. An attractive stone entrance to the camp site, with two 14’ aluminum gates were also donated by H.A. Stephens.

1960's

Travis Gibson was asked to be elected as Encampment President in 1960. During 1960, an inspirational center of “Praying Hands” was constructed . Three volleyball courts and two basketball courts were constructed. FBC Brady bought the Howard Payne College Lodge. Veribest Baptist church of Concho Valley Association built a cabin. A bookstore and concession building was constructed at a cost of less than $5,000. In 1963, Travis Gibson was elected to be the first Administrator of the camp. Other camp staff during this time included Clifford and Happy Nelson who operated the camp Trading Post in 1963. Mr. & Mrs. Lewis Stuckey operated the Trading Post in later years. In 1969, Mr. & Mrs. Hershal Barker were hired to live on the camp grounds and assist with the camp operations. In 1963, and eighteen foot deck boat was purchased for the purpose of recreation. It was paid for by a fee of .50 per passenger. Tradition of hot rolls in the dining hall started by Mrs. Sikes in 1964. In 1965, groundbreaking services for the Huff building were held November 6, 1965. In 1965, the camp was opened to churches of other denominations for the first time.

1970's

In 1971, the roads of the Encampment were topped with asphalt. Dr. & Mrs. Z.T. Huff gave the Encampment a gift of $27,750 for the complete remodeling of the FBC Brownwood cabin into a year round facility to accommodate 64 people. 24 tables and chairs were given by FBC Dublin. One of the first Senior Adult camps was held with Lewis Stuckey as director and Joe Trussell leading in music. In 1978, Bill Brown was hired as the second administrator of the camp. Paddleboats and canoes were added to the lakefront recreation.

1980's

The camp flag pole was given by Mr. & Mrs. Lewis Stuckey. The steeple on the tabernacle was donated by Mr. & Mrs. Dick Williams and Braswell Locker families of Brownwood. Mr. & Mrs. Jerry Callaway built a trailer park. The large bell in the courtyard, “The Roby Bell,” was given in memory of Mrs. A.E. Roby by her husband. In 1983, The new dorm was completed and named in honor of Rev. & Mrs. Travis Gibson, previous camp Administrator. A new house was built for the camp Administrator toward the front entrance of the camp. The camp office was also in built into the house. In 1984, The new Dining Hall was completed and named after the donor, Mr, & Mrs. Ruben Packer. In 1984, the first boat dock was built. In 1985, Roger and Trish Roulette were hired. Roger was hired for maintenance, and Trish worked in the kitchen and other areas at camp. The first issue of “The Challenger,” a camp newspaper with C.W. Christian, editor was published in 1986. In 1986 the seven room motel unit was constructed on the location of the old dining hall. In May of 1989, Marlin Felts was hired as the 3rd camp Administrator. The camp office was moved from the administrator’s home to the remodeled Lakeveiw cabin.

1990's

The first big flood which did substantial damage to the camp was in April of 1990. In 1990, the camp formed a committee of Capitol Funds Improvement for the purpose of a $300,000 fund drive. The money was to be used to build a Waste Water Treatment plant. Many renovations took place in the 1990’s including Coleman 1st cabin, Huff Building, dining hall floor replaced after the flood, handicap rails and ramps installed on dining hall sidewalks. The first expansion of the property was in 1991 when 300 acres of adjacent land was added at a cost of $300,000. During 1992, a building in Burkett, Texas was donated to be used by the camp. In 1995, construction began on the auditorium from the materials salvaged from the Burkett building. A wooden deck was added to the West side of the dining hall with a lakefront view. Volunteer, Mr. & Mrs. Fred Koffell did a lot of rock mason work including the front gate entrance, the front of the auditorium, fireplace in the Coleman 1st cabin, and the ornamental stage in the prayer garden. Disc Golf course was added. Staff added in the 1990’s include: Malcom and Mary Aaron, maintenance; Dorothy Roberts, Food Service, Tonya Lindeman, office assistant. Ruth Dumas joined the staff as a resident volunteer. Rhonda O’Banion was hired as summer staff in 1989, then office assistant in 1990. Vonda Williamson became the first full time salaried Food Service Manager. The family of C.W. Christian donated money toward the building of a childcare facility to be named in honor of their daughter, Louanne Christian Knight. The Melwood cabin was remodeled for this use. The auditorium was named in honor of Doil and Elva Hammons, volunteers, and was used for the first time in 1996.

2000's

Adopt-a-Cabin program established for the purpose of upgrading cabins and adding air conditioning. Aqua Jump purchased for the lake recreation. Inflatable Iceberg purchased for the lakefront. Rhonda O’Banion was hired as the 4th Administrator of the camp in April of 2001. 2001 - The original tabernacle was repurposed to be used as the camp concession stand. 2002- The BLOB and tower was added to the lakefront recreation. 2003 – The Brady dorm was remodeled to take the place of the motel unit that had flooded more than once. The new motel unit was named the Connally Motel after Rev. & Mrs. Dan Connally, DOM of Heart of Texas Association. 2003 – Edmond Blvd. Baptist Church of San Angelo donated a ropes course to the camp to be moved to the property. 2005 – The Louanne Christian Center was remodeled to be a 4 room motel unit. 2006 – The groundbreaking ceremony took place for a new Welcome Center to house the camp office and meeting facility. The REO Endowment was established for the purpose of providing funding for future camp maintenance needs. 2007- 146 Acres of the land that was purchased in 1991 was put on the market for sale. The land sold in December of 2007 for approximately $700,000 The Welcome Center was completed and debt free. 2008 – The old office was converted to the camp First Aid station. Disc Golf course added for camp recreation. Constitution and bylaws of the camp revised to reflect consolidated associations and to allow for at-large board members. Purchase of 2008 John Deere tractor Addition of Paintball for camp recreation. 2009 – Many roof repairs done because of major wind storm damage Many dorm floors recovered with tile instead of carpet.

2010's

2010 – New staff house built for assistant director Ballinger dorm removed and built covered pavilion on slab. Original tabernacle – concession area rennovated Began re-painting all camp buildings to match with a dark rust color and green trim. Upgraded mattresses and bunks in some dorms. 2011 – constructed man made pond Upgraded several toilets on camp. There was a nearby grassfire resulting in an evacuation of the camp during Preteen camp in June of 2011. There was no damage to the camp or any campers. 2011 – Astroturf installed on volleyball courts Two ga-ga ball pits built for camp recreation 2013 – Team 1946 established 2014 –Covered recreation pavilion constructed 80’x100’ Coleman 1st dorm remodeled; Dining hall remodeled with serving lines moved for self service and a new drink station: Camp shooting range relocated; some dorm showers upgraded with privacy curtains for dressing 2015 – Old concession building (original Comanche dorm) emptied of storage and torn down ; new housekeeping building constructed on slab of Hopewell cabin; Large diesel pump donated for the filling of the camp pond The camp obtained it’s 501(c)3 status Eagle Rock carved by Tom Dunn added to the camp landscape. 2016 – New outdoor bathroom added to the auditorium parking lot including showers; Pavilion built over the Comanche dorm slab: “One Way” tree carving by Rhonda Roberts: purchased new inflatable slide for pond; COM built new swings, gazebo, and benches; relocated the camp gift shop by the lakeshore Purchased 23 acres and a house in Shady Oaks for the purpose of housing the camp director. 2017 – Sale of 13 acres of camp property including the former director’s residence and barn. Sold to former Administrator, Marlin Felts. 2018 – Volunteers constructed a new outdoor fireplace by the camp store / smoothie shop Camp officially began using a DBA, Heart of Texas Camp & Retreat New Zip Tower was constructed Portable drinking stations and new ice machine added as a result of a grant. 2019 – Began a new event of the Fall Festival to showcase the new zip tower and new camp covered wagon to the area Dodgeball and human foosball cage build behind the rec pavilion Security camera added at the front entrance for camper safety. Upgraded dock; received donation of 5 portable buildings to become the WOW Village private cottages Began re-painting all camp buildings

2020's

2020 – Eagle tree carving by Rhonda Roberts in March 2020 COVID began affecting camp attendance in March 2020. We were recipients of a matching donation program for Camp Relief; we received a government grant through the Payroll Protection program, and began a large fund drive “Sustain Our Camp” in order to fund operations account due to slow summer because of COVID.
