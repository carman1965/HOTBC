In the Begining . . .

A committee was appointed at the Layman’s Jubilee meeting to seek out a location for a Baptist Encampment Gound. On August 26, 1946, the committee recommended the purchase of 26.21 acres of land on the shores of Lake Brownwood at a price of $6,890. The grounds were dedicated in 1947 with a crowd of about 1500 people attending. Original kitchen facility moved to camp property from Camp Bowie Tabernacle constructed 60’x100’ of native rock, concrete and steel with a seating capacity of 1000 at a cost of $7,300. FBC Comanche built a large BBQ pit near the shore line Men of Downtown Bible Class in Brownwood built a tower just outside the tabernacle and installed a large bell with clapper. Water system was donated and 24 ft. water tower with 200 barrel water tank Bathhouse with shower and restroom facilities and septic tank was installed.
