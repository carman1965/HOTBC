1946

The Beginning

A committee was appointed at the Layman’s Jubilee meeting to seek out a location for a Baptist Encampment Gound. On August 26, 1946, the committee recommended the purchase of 26.21 acres of land on the shores of Lake Brownwood at a price of $6,890.

1947

The grounds were dedicated in 1947 with a crowd of about 1500 people attending.

Original kitchen facility moved to camp property from Camp Bowie.

1948

Tabernacle constructed 60’x100’ of native rock, concrete and steel with a seating capacity of 1000 at a cost of $7,300. FBC Comanche built a large BBQ pit near the shore line Men of Downtown Bible Class in Brownwood built a tower just outside the tabernacle and installed a large bell with clapper. Water system was donated and 24 ft. water tower with 200 barrel water tank Bathhouse with shower and restroom facilities and septic tank was installed.

1950's

A home was built for a caretaker at a cost of $3,200. Girls Auxiliary and Royal Ambassador camps were started. Mr. & Mrs. Wild were employed as the first caretakers. In 1953, Mr. & Mrs. G.W. Howard took over as caretakers. H.A. Stephens of Brownwood built the first cabin and classroom. The first church to build a cabin was FBC Winters. T.M. Cash of Priddy, Texas built a cabin. WMU Lodge of District 16 was built at a cost of $6,000. Lodges were built by FBC Bangs, FBC Hamilton, FBC Santa Anna, and FBC Comanche. In order to provide all weather roads, crushed rock from Hall Mountain South of Brownwood was put down in 1952. Also, a concrete floor was put in the tabernacle and a pump pressure tank was installed for the water system. In 1952, a new addition to the dining room was built with screened sides and concrete floor to join the original kitchen. A 60’ x100’ swimming pool of concrete construction was built near the shoreline. Cabins were built by Lakeview Baptist Church, San Angelo and FBC DeLeon. In 1953, lodges were built by the following churches: FBC Brownwood, FBC San Angelo, Park Heights of San Angelo, FBC Early, FBC Eden, Mills County Association and Coleman County Association. In 1954, the following cabins were built: Cabins 2,3,4 & 5 were built by H.A. Stephens and were sold to Howard Payne College, Pumphrey Baptist Church, FBC Paint Rock, Melwood Baptist of Brownwood, Hopewell of Crews, FBC El Dorado. A cabin was moved to the grounds by Belle Plain Baptist of Brownwood. In 1955, cabins were built by FBC San Saba, FBC Ballinger, and Coggin Ave. Baptist of Brownwood. H.A. Stephens had 57 benches built for the camp. An attractive stone entrance to the camp site, with two 14’ aluminum gates were also donated by H.A. Stephens.
